Personal Statement

I am a graduate of the esteemed Universidad de Buenos Aires, the most prestigious university in Argentina, with a degree in formal linguistics and generative grammar. My academic journey also included a study of computational linguistics, which kindled my enthusiasm for programming and impelled me to pursue further studies in web development. I have also successfully completed a comprehensive course on Outlook and Web Development through LinkedIn. My proficiency extends to email management, social media engagement, and the comprehensive utilisation of Microsoft Office suite.

Presently, I am dedicated to advancing my expertise in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, with a steadfast aim of becoming a proficient full-stack developer. Beyond my academic pursuits, I have assumed the role of a private tutor, imparting knowledge to students in subjects including mathematics, syntax, philosophy, and history at the secondary education level. Additionally, I have conducted virtual Spanish lessons catering to students residing in the United States.

My overarching aspiration is to continue my journey of learning and evolution within the domains of linguistics and programming. I am invigorated by the myriad opportunities that lie ahead, propelling me to embrace them with anticipation and fervor.

As an ardent aficionado of English Literature, I harbor affection for several cherished books:

  • Dracula by Bram Stoker: This compelling narrative enthralled me during my adolescence, captivating my attention to the point of ceaseless engagement. Its extraordinary storyline rendered me incapable of putting it aside, resulting in its completion within a mere 10 days.
  • Life by Keith Richards: An unexpected parallel with Dracula unfolds within the pages of this autobiography. Despite initial reservations surrounding a guitarist's memoir, the text proved captivating. Keith Richards regales readers with tales that transcend expectations, infusing the narrative with delightful eccentricity and amusement. The arresting account commences with Keith Richards' improbable police chase, an unforeseen overture that instantly ensnares the reader's attention.
  • Robinson Crusoe by Daniel Defoe: This remarkable opus delves into multifaceted philosophical themes. I delved even deeper into its relatively obscure sequel. The text imparts invaluable life lessons, often delving into economic principles, paralleling the essence of Gulliver's Travels, another literary masterpiece.
  • The Fatal Conceit: The Errors of Socialism by Friedrich Von Hayek: This outstanding work, hailing from the erudite pen of Friedrich Von Hayek, constitutes an imperative read in the realm of economics. Within its pages, Von Hayek expertly critiques the hubris of politicians who assert their superiority in governing our lives. However, truth echoes in the realization that we alone are the true architects of our destinies.

These profound literary contributions, among others, have indelibly influenced my comprehension of both literature and economics. A relentless thirst for knowledge propels my quest for captivating literary works, an enthusiasm that crescendos with the forthcoming initiation of The Man Versus the State.
